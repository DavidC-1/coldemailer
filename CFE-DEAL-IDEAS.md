# Consulting-for-Equity Deal Ideas for Independent Self-Storage Operators

Source-blended notes drawing on Roland Frasier's CFE framework (`06-roland-frasier/`), the Radius+ Forecast 2026 industry data (Pinecone `self-storage-reports` index), and the AI-adoption research in [`03-operations/ai-technology-adoption.md`](../03-operations/ai-technology-adoption.md).

**Goal:** structures that let me start acquiring facilities and equity without writing big checks, leveraging my specific skill set — building AI agents and automation that eliminate manual operator work.

**Operator-language rule:** Owners don't care about "AI" or "agents" or "LLMs." They care about *missed calls converted into rentals, delinquency rolled back, leases processed in 2 minutes instead of 20, hours of their week given back, and NOI lifted*. Every deliverable below is translated into those outcomes.

---

## Why the Standard Frasier Formula Has to Bend

Roland's default CFE offer is essentially **$10K/month (or 10% of revenue, whichever is less) + 10% equity**. That works for info-products, agencies, and ecom brands grossing $5M–$50M with high gross margins and a single owner who can absorb a six-figure consulting line item.

Independent self-storage operators are a different animal:

- **Absolute dollars per facility are modest.** A typical single 30,000–50,000 sqft independent facility grosses $400K–$700K/yr. $120K/yr consulting is 17–30% of gross — they will never sign that.
- **The asset, not the operator's PnL, holds the value.** A facility worth $4–8M at a 6.5% cap on $260K–$520K NOI. Your leverage point is the **valuation lift**, not the operating cost line.
- **Owners are part-time, semi-retired, or family-run.** Many small operators are 55–75 years old. They don't think like growth-stage CEOs; they think like landlords. The pitch must speak landlord, not founder.
- **REIT pricing pressure is real.** Per the Radius+ 2026 Forecast, Extra Space and peers are running aggressive intro web rates to "buy" occupancy then ECRI-ing within months. This is squeezing independents who don't have the systems to match.
- **The fragmentation is huge.** Radius+ counts ~43,500–49,500 small private operators vs only ~9,000 public + ~6,500 large private (Industry Structure ch. 08). This is the addressable market.
- **They're allergic to tech jargon but love results.** Single-facility owners on Self-Storage Talk openly mock chatbots and AI hype (see ai-technology-adoption.md). But the same operators say yes immediately to "we will answer every call you currently miss and turn them into rentals." Pitch outcomes, never tech.

**So the framework I want to keep:** equity in exchange for services. **What I want to change:** fee structure flips from cash-heavy to performance-heavy, equity is sized to the value created, deal types segment by operator size and pain point, and every deliverable is described as an operator-felt outcome.

## The Economic Framework (Adjusted)

Three numbers to anchor every deal:

- **Baseline NOI snapshot** — frozen at engagement, audited by my team or a third party.
- **Stabilized cap rate for the market** — typically 6.0–7.5% for independent quality.
- **Value created** = (NOI lift × 1/cap) — this is what my equity is sized against.

Rule of thumb: **My total compensation (fees + equity-on-paper) should be ~20–30% of the value I create**, not a fixed dollar figure. That keeps the deal arithmetic honest and the operator sees the math.

Worked example: lift NOI from $300K to $360K on a 7% cap → $857K of value created. My target take across fees + equity ≈ $170K–$260K over 24–36 months.

**My personal filter:** I only do deals where modeled equity value at year 3–5 ≥ $150K. Anything below isn't worth the relationship cost.

## Standard Term-Sheet Elements (Apply to All Deals)

Every deal regardless of structure includes these baseline terms. Individual deals layer on top of this skeleton.

- **Baseline establishment:** Trailing-12 P&L audit, rent roll snapshot, occupancy snapshot, current ancillary attach rates, current delinquency aging, captured-call rate audit (I install call tracking week 1). All numbers signed off before any work begins.
- **Term:** 36 months default. 60 months for equity-heavy structures. 12-month minimum on any engagement.
- **IP ownership:** I retain ownership of any AI agents, codebases, prompts, models, and SOPs I build. Operator gets a perpetual license for use at their facilities during the term. License terminates if equity is wound down for cause. *This is non-negotiable — protects portability.*
- **Vesting:** Equity vests over the term with a 12-month cliff. Acceleration on change-of-control, refi above threshold, or operator default.
- **Information rights:** Monthly P&L, quarterly bank statements, annual third-party valuation. Operator agrees to maintain a single PMS as source of truth (per Capranos warning: "shadow spreadsheets are revenue-killers").
- **Drag-along, tag-along, ROFR.** Pre-negotiated.
- **Non-compete / non-circumvent:** Operator agrees not to retain my methods, vendors, or staff for follow-on facilities outside our agreement.
- **Termination for cause:** Either side can terminate with 90 days notice for material breach; equity treatment specified per deal type.
- **Performance kicker definition:** Always defined as *delta above baseline trailing-12*, never gross dollars. Audited annually by mutually-agreed third party.
- **PCI/data compliance:** Voice/chat agents handling payment data routed through PCI-DSS-compliant infrastructure; tenant data subject to a DPA.

### Deal #1 — Value-Creation Partnership (Performance-Tiered Fees + Equity)

**The default offer; lead with this for most engagements.**

**Who it's for:** Single-facility or 2–3 facility operators, owner reasonably engaged, willing to give up modest equity for outcomes they can measure.

**The pitch (one line):** "We add 15–25% to your NOI in 18 months by handling the things that quietly cost you money every day — missed calls, slow move-ins, late tenants, and rate increases you keep putting off. You pay us a base fee and a share of the lift, plus a small equity stake so we're on the same side of the table for the long haul."

**Deliverables (all from my AI-agent menu):**
- Voice + chat agent stack (inbound capture, after-hours, hold-and-hand-off)
- ECRI orchestration agent (rate increases on schedule with the right language)
- Insurance-attach agent at every move-in
- Delinquency roll-back agent
- Dynamic pricing rules engine (manual tiering first, ML later if data supports)
- Monthly performance review with the owner; quarterly business review

**Fee tiers by facility revenue:**

| Tier | Facility Gross Revenue | Base Fee | Performance Kicker | Equity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | <$500K | $2,500/mo | 25% of NOI lift above baseline | 8% common |
| Tier 2 | $500K–$1M | $4,000/mo | 25% of NOI lift above baseline | 10% common |
| Tier 3 | $1M+ (portfolio) | $7,000/mo | 30% of NOI lift above baseline | 12% common |

**Fee mechanics:**
- Base fee billed monthly, due net-15.
- Performance kicker calculated annually against audited delta-NOI, paid within 60 days of audit completion.
- Base fee discontinues at end of term; performance kicker continues for 36 months post-term as a "tail."

**Equity mechanics:**
- Common units in the facility LLC (or HoldCo if portfolio).
- 12-month cliff, then monthly vesting over remainder of 36-month term.
- Liquidity at next of: sale, refi above 75% LTV release threshold, recap, or year-7 buyout right at 14× trailing-12 NOI.
- Tag-along, drag-along, ROFR.

**Success metrics (defined upfront):**
- Captured-call rate ≥ 95% by month 3
- ECRI cycle executed within 60 days
- Insurance attach ≥ 50% by month 6
- Delinquency 30+ aging reduced ≥ 25% by month 12
- NOI delta ≥ 15% by month 18

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- If milestones missed by month 12, operator can terminate without penalty; my equity vested-to-date holds, no further vesting.
- If I terminate for cause, full vesting accelerates.

**Realistic objections + responses:**
- "10% is a lot." → "It's 10% of upside we create together. Your downside is the base fee. If we don't lift NOI, you keep your facility and we walk away with what we already vested for the work we already did."
- "What if I sell in year 2?" → "Then your sale price reflects our lift, you get 88–92% of it, and we get our equity share. We win when you win."

### Deal #2 — "Front Door" Implementation (The Foot-in-the-Door Offer)

**Reframed from "Tech & Marketing Stack" — now operator-language outcomes only.**

**Who it's for:** Skeptical operators, single-facility owners who won't sign a long-form CFE deal until they see proof. Also great for owners who think they "already have a system" and need a small win to recognize the gap.

**The pitch (one line):** "Give me 90 days and a flat fee to install a 24/7 front door for your facility — every call answered, every after-hours website visit converted, every lead followed up with. If it doesn't move the needle, you owe me nothing extra and you keep what we built."

**Deliverables:**
- Voice agent on the main line (hold-and-hand-off pattern — does NOT try to close, just captures + hands to owner)
- Web chat agent on the facility site with PMS integration for unit availability + pricing
- SMS lead-nurture for every quoted-but-not-rented lead
- Google Business Profile cleanup + automated review-response engine
- Weekly captured-call/conversion report

**Fee mechanics:**
- $1,500–$2,500/mo base (depending on facility size)
- 5% of *incremental* revenue above trailing-12 baseline, paid quarterly
- 12-month minimum, auto-renews unless either side gives 60-day notice

**Equity mechanics:**
- **5–7% common units, vesting only if operator signs into Deal #1 at month 12**, OR
- Alternative: 3–5% equity vesting immediately if operator commits to 36-month term upfront

**Success metrics:**
- Captured-call rate ≥ 95% by month 2
- After-hours web-chat conversions ≥ 2/mo by month 3
- GBP profile views up ≥ 30% by month 6

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- 90-day money-back window: if captured-call rate doesn't hit 90% by day 90, operator pays only platform costs and keeps the install.
- Any vested equity stays; future vesting paused.

**Realistic objections:**
- "I already have an answering service." → "Answering services log messages. We book rentals. Show me a month and I'll show you how many rentals you're missing."
- "I don't trust AI talking to my customers." → Reference ai-technology-adoption.md operator quote: "We don't try to close. We answer the basic questions and hand them to you within 60 seconds." That framing converts skeptics.

### Deal #3 — Pure Performance Revenue Recovery (Zero Cash Out)

**Who it's for:** Operators who refuse any monthly fee on principle, or who are cash-constrained. Especially attractive to distressed lease-up owners.

**The pitch (one line):** "I do the work, you pay me nothing up front. I take half of every extra dollar I produce for 36 months and a small piece of the upside on the back end. If I don't produce, I don't get paid."

**Deliverables:**
- ECRI orchestration agent
- Insurance-attach agent (this alone often pays for the whole engagement)
- Late-fee + admin-fee discipline review and automated enforcement
- Delinquency roll-back agent
- Quarterly P&L review with owner

**Fee mechanics:**
- **Zero base fee.**
- **50% of NOI lift above baseline** for 36 months, paid quarterly.
- Operator covers platform/SaaS pass-throughs (typically $400–$900/mo).

**Equity mechanics:**
- 5% common units, vesting only if NOI lift exceeds 12% over baseline by month 18 (success-gated vesting).
- Stake held in a SAFE-like instrument until vesting condition met.

**Success metrics:**
- Insurance attach: 20% → 60%+ within 9 months
- ECRI executed on full book within 6 months
- Delinquency aging 30+: down ≥ 25% within 12 months
- NOI delta ≥ 12% by month 18 (equity vesting trigger)

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- If NOI delta < 8% by month 18: I keep fees earned, no equity vests, deal winds down at month 24.
- If owner sells before month 36: I get 50% of trailing-12 NOI-lift kicker as buyout, plus any vested equity.

**Realistic objections:**
- "50% feels like a lot of any new dollar." → "Half of something is infinitely more than 100% of nothing. The other 50% goes straight to your bottom line and your valuation grows with it."
- "What if you cherry-pick easy wins and leave?" → "Term is locked at 36 months and I'm tying equity to sustained lift, not a one-time bump."

### Deal #4 — Acquisition Catalyst (Sweat-Equity Deal Sourcing)

**Who it's for:** Capital-rich operators with 1+ facility who want to grow but lack deal-flow time or network.

**The pitch (one line):** "I find you facilities to buy. I underwrite them. I bring you closed deals. You pay nothing until a deal closes — and when it does, I take 25% equity in that new facility and you keep operational control."

**Deliverables:**
- Off-market deal sourcing (cold outreach, broker network, distressed/maturing-debt lists)
- Underwriting model and BOV for every shortlisted deal
- LOI drafting, financing introductions, third-party diligence coordination
- AI-agent stack installed at each new acquired facility from day 1 (capture, ECRI, insurance attach, delinquency)
- Post-close 90-day stabilization plan

**Fee mechanics:**
- Zero monthly fee.
- 1.5% acquisition fee at close (covers my diligence costs; capped at $50K per deal).
- No fee on deals that don't close.

**Equity mechanics:**
- **20–30% common equity in the SPV for each closed facility.**
- Tiering: 20% if I'm just sourcing/underwriting; 25% if I'm also installing the AI-agent stack post-close; 30% if I'm bringing capital partners as well.
- Operator funds the equity check; my equity is for sweat + sourcing + systems.
- Standard waterfall: 8% pref to operator's contributed equity, then 70/30 (operator/me) above pref.

**Success metrics:**
- Pipeline volume: ≥ 25 underwritten deals/yr
- Closure rate: ≥ 2 closed deals/yr
- Post-close stabilization: trailing-3 NOI ≥ underwriting model within 12 months

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Each SPV exits on its own timeline; I don't have aggregate-portfolio exit rights unless we agree to a HoldCo wrap (see Deal #10).
- If no deals close in 12 months, agreement renegotiates or terminates.

**Realistic objections:**
- "25% is a lot for sourcing." → "It's 25% of a deal that wouldn't exist without me. You're putting up 100% of the capital but you're getting 75% of an asset you wouldn't have found, with my systems running it from day 1."
- "Can I just hire a broker?" → "Brokers bring you on-market deals everyone else has seen. I bring off-market deals and the operational engine to make them work."

### Deal #5 — Distressed Lease-Up Rescue (High-Equity, High-Lift)

**Who it's for:** Owners of 2022–2024 deliveries still bleeding cash, lease-up stalled below 60%, debt service tight. Per Radius+ supply chapter, this cohort is sizable and visible.

**The pitch (one line):** "Your lease-up is stalled, your debt service is squeezing you, and selling now means accepting a haircut. I bring the operational turnaround and (if needed) a capital partner — in exchange for a meaningful equity stake. You keep the asset, avoid the forced sale, and ride the recovery."

**Deliverables:**
- Full operational takeover under a management agreement
- Aggressive lease-up marketing blitz (PPC, GBP, paid local, referral)
- AI voice/chat agents capturing 100% of inbound
- Insurance attach + ancillary stack from day 1
- Optional: I broker a bridge capital partner from my network
- Possible manager replacement (this is often where the leakage is)

**Fee mechanics:**
- Base management fee: 5% of revenue (matches third-party-management market rate per Radius+ data).
- Lease-up bonus: $200 per net new tenant above month-baseline (capped at $30K/yr).
- No NOI kicker — the equity is the kicker.

**Equity mechanics:**
- **25–35% common equity** depending on distress level:
  - 25% for occupancy 50–60% and current on debt
  - 30% for occupancy 40–50% and stressed debt service
  - 35% for occupancy <40% and missed payments
- Vesting: 50% at signing (we earn it by taking the risk), 50% pro-rata over 36 months.
- Owner retains majority and operational vote on disposition.

**Success metrics:**
- Occupancy ≥ 80% within 18 months
- DSCR ≥ 1.25× within 12 months
- Stabilized NOI ≥ debt service + 15% buffer by month 24

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- If owner sells once stabilized, I get my equity share at market value.
- If lease-up still stalled at month 24: owner has put option to force my exit at vested-equity book value; I have call option to acquire the facility at trailing-12 NOI × 13×.

**Realistic objections:**
- "30% equity for a turnaround?" → "30% of an asset that's recovering vs 100% of an asset that's about to foreclose. Run the math on alternative outcomes."
- "What if you can't fix it?" → "Put option built in. You can force me out at year 2 if I don't perform, with no continuing obligation."

### Deal #6 — Operator-as-Manager Joint Venture

**Who it's for:** Operators who want to remain owners but recognize they don't have the bandwidth or systems to maximize the asset. Often boomer owners with 1–2 facilities they want to keep but step back from.

**The pitch (one line):** "We form a 50/50 partnership on the facility's *upside.* You keep your contributed equity and a guaranteed pref return; I run the facility with my team and systems; we split the value lift on the back end."

**Deliverables:**

- Quarterly investor-style reporting
- Sale/refi positioning at year 4–5

**Fee mechanics:**
- 4% of revenue (below standard 6% TPM fee — the discount is paid in equity).
- $10K project-management fee per material capex project.

**Equity mechanics:**
- Owner contributes facility at agreed-fair-value into JV LLC.
- Owner gets preferred equity = contributed value × 8% pref.
- I get common/promote equity:
  - 8% pref to operator's contributed equity
  - Next: 100% to operator until 100% of contributed equity returned
  - Above that: 60/40 (operator/me) on ongoing distributions
  - On sale: 30% of *value lift above contributed value* to me before pro-rata split, then 60/40 above that

**Success metrics:**

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Year-5 sale required unless both parties agree to extend.

**Realistic objections:**

- AI-agent operational stack to support new DSCR
- Loan-document negotiation support
- Post-refi operational management or oversight (per operator preference)

**Fee mechanics:**
- $25K refinance success fee at closing (credited back if equity fully vests)
- Optional 2%/yr revenue share if I'm operating post-refi
- No monthly fee otherwise

**Equity mechanics:**
- **15–25% common equity** depending on capital and risk:
  - 15% if I'm only sourcing the capital
  - 20% if sourcing capital + installing AI-agent ops stack
  - 25% if also providing capital from my own network as a participation
- Vesting: 50% at refi close, 50% pro-rata over 36 months.
- Standard ROFR/tag/drag.

**Success metrics:**
- Refi closes within 120 days of engagement
- New DSCR ≥ 1.30× achieved within 18 months
- Stabilized NOI supports debt service + capex reserve

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- If refi doesn't close within 180 days: agreement terminates, I'm paid only out-of-pocket costs (max $10K).
- If post-refi DSCR drops below 1.10× and stays for 6 months: I have right to install full management or trigger sale process.

**Realistic objections:**
- "I'd rather sell than give up equity." → "Selling at the trough means a discount of 15–25%. Giving up 20% equity to keep the asset and ride the recovery is the math-positive choice."
- "Where does your capital come from?" → "Family offices and private debt funds focused on storage. I bring intros, not blind pools. You sign with them, not me."

### Deal #10 — Multi-Facility Platform Equity (The Roll-Up Setup)

**Who it's for:** Operators with 3–5 facilities who want to grow but lack institutional polish, capital network, or deal flow.

**The pitch (one line):** "Your three facilities are worth $X separately. Wrapped as a professional platform with consistent operations, institutional reporting, and a growth plan, they're worth meaningfully more on exit. I help you build the platform and take an equity stake in the wrap, not the underlying facilities."

**Deliverables:**
- HoldCo formation, structuring, legal coordination
- Consistent operations rollout across all facilities (AI-agent stack at each)
- Centralized reporting, banking, treasury
- Group purchasing (insurance, supplies, vendors)
- Deal-flow pipeline for HoldCo acquisitions
- Institutional-buyer relationship building (PE, family offices, REIT M&A)
- Year-3 to year-5 exit positioning

**Fee mechanics:**
- $5K/mo HoldCo management retainer
- 1.5% acquisition fee on any HoldCo acquisition
- 3% disposition fee on HoldCo exit (capped per side)

**Equity mechanics:**
- **10–15% of HoldCo common equity** (not underlying facility-level equity).
- Promote structure: 8% pref to operator's contributed equity, then 80/20 above pref to 2× MOIC, then 70/30 thereafter.
- 5-year minimum hold.

**Success metrics:**
- HoldCo NOI ≥ 25% above sum-of-facilities baseline within 36 months
- Platform multiple uplift: aim for 0.5–1.0 percentage-point cap-rate compression on exit
- At least 1 add-on acquisition closed per year of engagement

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Year-5 sale process required unless both parties extend.
- Failed exit at year-5: operator buyout right at appraisal.
- Operator's contributed equity has senior return rights through the waterfall.

**Realistic objections:**
- "I don't need a holdco — my three facilities run fine." → "Today they do. But three facilities sold separately get retail buyers at retail cap rates. A three-facility platform with institutional reporting gets institutional buyers at institutional cap rates. That's typically 50–100 bps of cap-rate compression, which on $5M of NOI is $7–15M of additional sale value."
- "What if your acquisitions hurt my existing facilities?" → "Every acquisition has its own underwriting and is approved by both of us. Veto on either side. No forced add-ons."

### Deal #11 — Labor Reduction Equity (AI Eliminates the Site Manager)

**Reframed from "AI Automation Implementation" — now operator-language outcomes only.**

**Who it's for:** Operators paying $60K–$100K/yr for a site manager whose tasks are 70%+ automatable. Especially attractive to owners of facilities where the manager is retiring, underperforming, or expensive relative to facility revenue.

**The pitch (one line):** "Your site manager costs you $80K/yr. I can replace 70% of what they do with AI — calls answered, leases signed, late tenants chased, units released, books closed — and either you keep them at half the role or you let them go. Either way you save $40K+ per facility per year, and I take half of that savings for 24 months plus a small equity stake."

**Deliverables:**
- Voice + chat agent stack
- Document processing (leases, IDs, insurance)
- Automated move-in / move-out workflow
- Delinquency + auction prep automation
- AP / invoice categorization
- Automated daily/weekly owner reporting
- Smart-access integration (if facility has compatible system)
- Optional: roving "district manager" coverage from my team for physical tasks the AI can't do (cleaning, lock cuts, occasional in-person tenant interactions)

**Fee mechanics:**
- **50% of audited labor savings** for 24 months. Audit = (prior site-manager fully-loaded cost) − (post-AI fully-loaded cost including SaaS pass-throughs and any roving coverage).
- Setup fee: $20K (one-time, covers install + training).

**Equity mechanics:**
- **5–8% common equity** vesting over 36 months.
- Equity vesting accelerates if labor savings exceed $50K/yr for 12 consecutive months.

**Success metrics:**
- Labor cost reduction ≥ $40K/yr per facility within 12 months
- Operational SLAs maintained: response time, captured-call rate, vacate turnover time
- Tenant Net-Promoter or review rating unchanged or improved

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- 90-day satisfaction window: if operational SLAs drop materially, operator can roll back and owes only setup fee and SaaS pass-throughs.
- At month 24, fee structure ends; operator owns the savings outright (subject to ongoing equity stake and SaaS subscriptions).

**Realistic objections:**
- "I don't want to fire my long-time manager." → "You don't have to. We re-scope the role to focus on the things that need a human: facility walks, tenant relationships, cleaning, marketing partnerships. The AI handles the routine. You may even keep them at the same pay if you want — the savings come from not having to hire a second person or pay overtime."
- "What if the AI screws up a lease?" → "Document processing has a human-in-loop checkpoint. Nothing legally binding goes through without manager review. We're automating the busywork, not the judgment calls."

### Deal #12 — NOI Guarantee with Equity Upside (Bold Risk-Reversal)

**Who it's for:** Skeptical operators who've been pitched before and are tired of consultants who don't deliver. Best used after I have ≥3 case studies with audited results.

**The pitch (one line):** "I guarantee a 15% NOI lift in 18 months. If I miss, you get every dollar of fees back. If I hit, I get my fees plus a 10% equity stake. The risk is on me."

**Deliverables:**
- Full Deal #1 deliverables (voice/chat/ECRI/insurance/delinquency/pricing)
- Quarterly third-party-audited NOI review
- Bond/escrow arrangement to back the guarantee (see fee mechanics)

**Fee mechanics:**
- Standard Tier-2 fees: $4K/mo base + 25% of NOI lift kicker
- Refund condition: if 18-month NOI delta < 15%, 100% of base fees refunded (kicker not applicable). Refund backed by an escrow or insurance bond I put up at engagement.
- Setup fee: $25K (covers bond/escrow funding cost, non-refundable)

**Equity mechanics:**
- **10% common equity** vesting only on hitting the 15% NOI lift threshold.
- If threshold hit at month 18: full equity vests immediately.
- If missed: zero equity vests.

**Success metrics:**
- NOI delta ≥ 15% by month 18 (binary trigger)
- Audited by mutually-agreed CPA firm

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Hard refund condition at month 18 — no extensions, no excuses
- If hit: standard Deal #1 mechanics apply for remainder of term
- If missed: refund executed, no equity, no further obligation either side

**Realistic objections:**
- "Sounds too good to be true." → "It's not for everyone — I only offer it to operators where I'm confident the baseline diagnostics show 15% is achievable. Let me run a 2-week diagnostic; if I don't see the path, I won't offer this structure."
- "Why would you give me a refund right?" → "Because I've done this enough times to know what's achievable. The refund right is what gets you to say yes. If I'm right, we both win. If I'm wrong, you didn't pay for hot air."

### Deal #13 — Sweat-Equity Sourcing (Reverse of Standard CFE)

**Who it's for:** Capital-rich operators (HNW, family offices, existing portfolio operators) who lack only deal flow.

**The pitch (one line):** "You have the capital and the operations team. You just need deals. I bring you closed facilities — no consulting fees, no monthly retainer. I take 10% equity in every deal I source that closes."

**Deliverables:**
- Off-market deal pipeline (cold outreach, distressed/maturing-debt lists, broker network, owner-direct)
- Underwriting model and BOV for every shortlisted deal
- LOI through close coordination
- Optional: AI-agent stack install at each new facility for an additional 5% equity

**Fee mechanics:**
- Zero cash fees.
- 1% origination fee at close (covers diligence costs; capped at $40K/deal)

**Equity mechanics:**
- **10% common equity in each closed-deal SPV.**
- Vesting: 100% at close (because the work is done at that point).
- Operator funds the equity check; my equity is for sourcing only.

**Success metrics:**
- Pipeline volume: ≥ 30 underwritten deals/yr
- Closure rate: ≥ 3 closed deals/yr
- Quality bar: every shortlisted deal must meet operator's stated investment criteria

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Each SPV exits per its own terms; I don't have aggregate exit rights.
- If no deals close in 12 months, agreement terminates; any deals I previously sourced still pay equity per terms.

**Realistic objections:**
- "Why would I give 10% on something I'm fully funding?" → "Because without me, you don't have the deal at all. Brokers bring you on-market deals at retail prices. I bring you off-market deals at material discounts. Your 90% on an off-market deal is worth more than your 100% on a deal you couldn't find."
- "Can I just hire you as a buyer's broker?" → "Brokers transact and walk away. I'm building a portfolio with you — the equity stake means I keep finding you deals for years, not one and done."

### Deal #14 — Operator Academy + Equity License (Scalable Horizontal)

**Who it's for:** Operators who want self-serve transformation, not consulting. Often DIY-mindset owners with 1–3 facilities who can implement themselves with the right playbook.

**The pitch (one line):** "I license you the entire playbook — every SOP, every AI agent, every vendor contract, every pricing rule, every marketing template. You implement at your own pace with quarterly support from my team. Small monthly fee, small equity stake — and you keep the operations in-house."

**Deliverables:**
- Full SOP library (every operational process documented)
- AI-agent stack licensed for self-serve install (voice, chat, ECRI, insurance, delinquency, AP)
- Vendor contract templates (insurance, retail, marketing, smart access)
- Pricing rules and ECRI scripts
- Marketing templates (GBP, paid search, SMS, email)
- Quarterly group office hours with my team
- Quarterly 1:1 review with each licensee

**Fee mechanics:**
- $1,500–$2,000/mo per facility license fee
- Setup fee: $7,500 (covers initial install assistance)
- 36-month minimum

**Equity mechanics:**
- **3–5% common equity in each facility being operated under the license.**
- Vesting over 36 months.
- Stake held in a SAFE-like instrument; converts on liquidity event or year-5 buyout right.

**Success metrics:**
- License usage: agents and SOPs in active use (measured via platform telemetry)
- NOI growth ≥ 8% over baseline within 24 months (lower bar than consulting because operator is doing the work)

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- Either side can terminate license at month 36 with 90-day notice; equity vested at termination remains.
- If operator stops using the platform but doesn't terminate: equity continues vesting (no clawback).

**Realistic objections:**
- "I'd rather just buy your software outright." → "It's not software — it's a system that includes ongoing updates, agent training data, and vendor relationships. The license keeps you current. And the equity component means we keep iterating on what makes your facility more valuable."
- "5% is more than I'd pay a consultant." → "Consultants leave. This is a 5-year compounding relationship. Run the math on what 5% of your future facility value is worth in 5 years vs paying for one consulting engagement."

### Deal #15 — Acquisition Option (Deferred Equity Right)

**Who it's for:** Owners who are considering selling but uncertain about timing. Often boomer owners "thinking about" selling in 1–3 years.

**The pitch (one line):** "I don't take equity today. Instead, I work with you for a small fee, lift your NOI, and lock in the right to buy your facility at a pre-agreed price within 24 months. If you sell to someone else, you owe me a kill fee. If you sell to me, you get a clean exit at a price we agreed to when we shook hands."

**Deliverables:**
- Full Deal #1 deliverables at discounted fee (voice/chat/ECRI/insurance/delinquency)
- Quarterly performance review with owner
- Standing exit-readiness work (clean books, organized records, transferable systems)
- Documented purchase option

**Fee mechanics:**
- $2,000/mo base + 15% of NOI lift kicker
- No equity in operating company

**Option mechanics (this is the "equity"):**
- **Right of First Refusal + Purchase Option** documented in option agreement
- Strike price: 14× trailing-12 NOI at time of option exercise (implies ~7.1% cap rate to me as buyer)
- Option exercisable any time within 24 months of execution
- Kill fee if owner sells to third party: $50K per facility (covers my opportunity cost + fees-foregone)
- Owner retains right to reject any third-party offer; my option is at the agreed multiple, not market

**Success metrics:**
- NOI delta ≥ 10% by month 18
- Facility records and systems exit-ready by month 12
- Option exercised or expired cleanly at month 24

**Exit / failure mechanics:**
- If I exercise: standard purchase agreement at the agreed multiple, 60-day close.
- If I don't exercise: option expires at month 24, no penalty either side, all prior fees retained.
- If owner sells to third party during option period: kill fee paid.

**Realistic objections:**
- "What if my facility is worth more in 2 years?" → "Then we both win — you bring in a higher multiple in the open market, I get my kill fee. The option just gives us both a baseline."
- "Why would I sell to you when I could test the market?" → "You can. I'm not blocking you. The option just guarantees you a price floor and a known buyer. If you don't like the price at year 2, you walk away and I get my kill fee. You're paying $50K for the option to lock in a buyer if the market gets worse."

## The Pitch Framework

For first conversations, lead with the **pain point**, not the offer. Per the Radius+ data and Dave Deems' quote, the line that opens doors is:

> "Most independent operators are realizing they don't have the systems or scale to compete with the REITs in this environment. I help operators close that gap without selling out — usually by adding 15–25% to NOI within 18 months. Want to see what that would look like on your facility?"

**Sequencing rules:**

1. **Diagnostic first, offer second.** Before pitching any deal, do a 2-week diagnostic: pull call logs (find captured-call rate), audit ECRI history, audit insurance attach, audit delinquency aging. Present findings as dollars left on the table.
2. **Lead with the smallest fitting deal.** Deal #2 (Front Door) or #11 (Labor Reduction) are the easiest yeses. Use them to prove value, then expand into #1.
3. **Never say "AI" in the first meeting.** Say "we answer every call," "we collect from late tenants," "we lift NOI." The technology is plumbing — they care about the water.
4. **Show the math early.** Operators are landlords. They respond to dollars-and-cents arithmetic, not promise narratives.
5. **Anchor equity to value created, not company value.** "We create $X of value, we take $Y of it" is a fairness frame they understand.

## Realistic Equity Targets

Reasonable equity stake sizing (for my own deal-pricing discipline):

- **Single small facility (consulting/operating):** 5–10% — modest upside, easy yes
- **Single mid facility (deeper engagement):** 10–15% — better upside, slightly harder yes
- **Multi-facility portfolio (HoldCo):** 10–15% — biggest dollar upside, hardest yes
- **Distressed / rescue:** 25–35% — earned because the alternative is worse for operator
- **Succession bridge:** 50%+ over time — earned because I'm replacing the owner functionally
- **Acquisition Catalyst per deal:** 20–30% — earned because the deal wouldn't exist without me
- **Sourcing-only per deal:** 10% — earned because the deal wouldn't exist without me, but operator funds and operates

## Risk Mitigation Notes

- **Always anchor to a baseline NOI snapshot** with audited financials. Without it, the NOI-lift kicker is unenforceable.
- **Vesting cliffs matter.** 12-month cliff, then monthly. Operators will try to terminate before equity vests if they sense early lift.
- **Drag-along, tag-along, ROFR** in every operating agreement.
- **Information rights.** Monthly P&L, quarterly bank statements, annual third-party valuation.
- **IP retention.** I own the AI agents, prompts, models, codebase. Operator gets a license. This protects portability across the portfolio I'm building.
- **Single source of truth on data.** Per Capranos (ai-technology-adoption.md): "Shadow spreadsheets are revenue-killers." Make consolidated PMS a contractual requirement.
- **PCI compliance** on any voice/chat agents handling payment data. Non-negotiable.
- **Non-compete + non-circumvent** on operators I source deals into.
- **Tenant data DPA** in place before agents go live.
- **Human-in-loop on legally-binding documents.** Leases, auction notices — AI drafts, human signs.
- See [equity-due-diligence-checklist.md](../06-roland-frasier/equity-due-diligence-checklist.md) for the broader Frasier diligence list.

## Open Questions to Answer Before Going to Market

1. Do I want a portfolio of many small stakes (deals #13, #14) or a concentrated portfolio of fewer deep stakes (deals #1, #6, #10)?
2. What's my own first-loss capital budget for deals #5 and #9, where capital is implied?
3. Which operator persona do I have the most warm-relationship access to right now? That dictates which 2–3 deal structures to lead with this quarter.
4. What's my legal template stack for each deal type? Need a forms library before pitching — Frasier templates need self-storage-specific addenda.
